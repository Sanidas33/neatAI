Unlocking New Flavors: How Enzymes Are Revolutionizing Cocktail Syrups

The world of mixology is evolving—and bartenders are increasingly turning to nature’s own catalysts to create extraordinary cocktail syrups. Imagine transforming a humble pastry into a liquid gold syrup, naturally sweetened and brimming with depth. Today, we explore how two powerful enzymes—amylase and glucosidase—can work together to convert the starches in a bombolone (or any starchy treat) into a sophisticated syrup that elevates your favorite drinks.

Understanding Enzyme Action

Amylase: Operating best at temperatures between 55°C and 70°C (131°F to 158°F) in slightly acidic conditions (ideal pH 4.0–4.5), amylase initiates the breakdown of long-chain starch molecules into smaller fragments—maltose and dextrins.

Glucosidase: Working hand-in-hand with amylase, glucosidase further disassembles these dextrins into individual glucose molecules, boosting natural sweetness and adding rich complexity.

(While pectinase is also used to break down pectin in food processing, in our cocktail syrup recipe we focus solely on amylase and glucosidase.)

Crafting the Bombolone Syrup

Imagine taking a bombolone—a light, airy doughnut—and transforming it into a syrup that captures its essence. Here’s how:

Ingredients:

  • 250g bombolone (or your preferred starchy pastry)
  • 250g water
  • 12.5g amylase
  • 12.5g glucosidase

Procedure:

  1. Blend for Maximum Interaction: Combine the bombolone and water in a high-powered blender until smooth.
  2. Enzyme Infusion: Add amylase and glucosidase; mix thoroughly.
  3. Precision Cooking: Vacuum-seal the mixture and cook using a sous vide or controlled water bath at 55°C for 2 hours.
  4. Strain and Refine: Strain through a fine-mesh sieve (or SuperBag) to remove solids.
  5. Cool and Store: Refrigerate the resulting syrup for later use in cocktails.

The Science Behind the Sweetness

During the infusion process, amylase cleaves starch chains into maltose and dextrins. Glucosidase then breaks these down into glucose units—yielding a naturally sweet syrup without added sugars.

Innovations in Modern Mixology

  • Natural Sweetness: Extracts sweetness directly from the pastry.
  • Enhanced Flavor Complexity: Releases a spectrum of sugars that enrich the flavor profile.
  • Creative Inspiration: Opens up new possibilities for cocktail creation.

Conclusion

Harnessing the power of amylase and glucosidase in cocktail making takes mixology to a new level—transforming everyday ingredients into extraordinary syrups that deliver natural sweetness and complexity. Embrace enzyme-driven techniques and unlock a world of nuanced flavors in your cocktails!
