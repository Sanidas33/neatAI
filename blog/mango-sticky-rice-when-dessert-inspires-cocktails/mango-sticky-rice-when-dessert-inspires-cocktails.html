Mango Sticky Rice: When Dessert Inspires Cocktails

By Drinks By Neat

As a bartender, I find inspiration everywhere, but the culinary world is a constant source of ideas waiting to be translated into drinks. Mango Sticky Rice is an iconic dessert for a reason – the balance of textures, tropical flavors, and comforting sweetness make it ripe for cocktail exploration.

The Challenge: Sticky Rice in a Glass

Many of the world's best bars have tackled Mango Sticky Rice-inspired cocktails, often focusing on replicating the fresh mango element. This time, I wanted a different approach. Could I capture the essence of the sticky rice itself in a creamy, complex drink with those familiar tropical vibes?

Harnessing Enzymes for Texture & Flavor

My secret weapon? Enzymes. Remember those posts about the rice cream? Here's where it pays off! A bit of science:

  • Amylase: Breaks down the rice's starches into smaller sugars and dextrins, adding a hint of natural sweetness and starting to create that luscious texture.
  • Glucosidase: Converts those dextrins into glucose – a simple, very sweet sugar. The result is a creamy base with subtle sweetness, free of any added sugar.

The Cocktail: Creamy, Tropical, and Unexpected

The rice cream forms the heart of the drink, supported by:

  • Mango-Infused Planteray Rum: Spice and fruitiness play beautifully with the rice cream.
  • Brooklyn Kura Sake No. 14: Delicate and dry, it balances the sweetness and adds complexity.
  • Coconut Water: A touch of freshness.

The result is a creamy, mango-forward cocktail that's reminiscent of the dessert, but with its own sophisticated twist. It's surprisingly balanced and absolutely delicious.

Recipe:

Mango Sticky Rice Cocktail (reminiscent of a Piña Colada)

  • 50ml Planteray Pineapple infused with freeze dried mango
  • 20ml Brooklyn Kura 14
  • 20ml Coconut Water
  • 40ml Rice cream

Method:

  1. Add the ingredients in a shaker.
  2. Shake and strain into a hurricane glass over ice.

Planteray Mango Infusion

Ingredients:

  • 700ml Planteray Pineapple
  • 200g Freeze dried mango

Method:

  1. Mix ingredients in a vacuum bag and seal.
  2. Cook Sous Vide at 55°C for 2 hours.
  3. Strain through a superbag.
  4. Bottle.

Rice Cream

Ingredients:

  • 250g Glutinous Rice
  • 1.5L Coconut milk (divided)
  • 12.5g Amylase
  • 12.5g Glucosidase

Method:

  1. Add 1L coconut milk in a pot over medium heat.
  2. Just before boiling, add the rice and reduce to a simmer.
  3. Cook for 20 mins.
  4. Let it cool to 55°C, blend and add the enzymes with the rest of the milk.
  5. Add in a vacuum bag and cook sous vide at 55°C for 4 hours.
  6. Strain through a super bag.
  7. Bottle and refrigerate.

This experiment is proof that even familiar dishes can spark unique cocktail creations. By looking at ingredients in new ways, and understanding a bit of the science behind them, we can push the boundaries of what's possible behind the bar.
